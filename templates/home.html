Ratio Value What is this?

Mean Return

 XX.XX A mean return (also known as expected return) is the estimated profit or loss an investor expects to achieve from a portfolio of investments.

Beta

 XX.XX Beta is a measure of the volatility–or systematic risk–of a security or portfolio compared to the market as a whole.

Jensen’s Alpha

 XX.XX Alpha refers to excess returns earned on an investment above the benchmark return. (This ones fake)

Sharpe Ratio

 XX.XX The Sharpe ratio adjusts a portfolio’s past performance—or expected future performance—for the excess risk that was taken by the investor.

Treynor Ratio

 XX.XX The Treynor ratio is a risk/return measure that allows investors to adjust a portfolio's returns for systematic risk.

R-squared

 XX.XX In investing, R-squared is generally interpreted as the percentage of a fund or security's movements that can be explained by movements in a benchmark index.

Information Ratio (IR)

 XX.XX IR is a measurement of portfolio returns above the returns of a benchmark, usually an index such as the S&P 500, to the volatility of those returns.

Modigliani Ratio (M2 Ratio)

 XX.XX M2 shows the return on an investment adjusted for risk in comparison to a benchmark.

Sortino Ratio

 XX.XX The Sortino ratio differs from the Sharpe ratio in that it only considers the standard deviation of the downside risk, rather than that of the entire (upside + downside) risk.

Maximum Drawdown

 XX.XX MDD is a measure of an asset's largest price drop from a peak to a trough.

Value At Risk (Var) - 90%

 XX.XX VaR is a way to quantify the risk of potential losses for an investment (90% Confidence Level).

Value At Risk (Var) - 95%

 XX.XX VaR is a way to quantify the risk of potential losses for an investment (95% Confidence Level).

Value At Risk (Var) - 99%

 XX.XX VaR is a way to quantify the risk of potential losses for an investment (99% Confidence Level).
